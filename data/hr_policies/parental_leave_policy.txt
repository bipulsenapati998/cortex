1. POLICY STATEMENT

Meridian Technologies is committed to supporting employees through significant life events, including the birth, adoption, or surrogacy of a child. This policy establishes a comprehensive parental leave program designed to allow employees to bond with and care for a new child while maintaining financial stability and job security.

2. ELIGIBILITY

To qualify for parental leave benefits under this policy, employees must meet the following criteria:

- Be classified as a full-time employee (working 30+ hours per week)
- Have completed a minimum of 12 months of continuous employment with Meridian Technologies at the time leave commences
- Be in good standing (not subject to an active Performance Improvement Plan or disciplinary action at the time of the leave request)

Part-time employees and contractors are not eligible for paid parental leave under this policy, though they may qualify for unpaid leave in accordance with applicable law.

3. LEAVE ENTITLEMENTS

3.1 Primary Caregiver Leave

Employees designated as the primary caregiver — typically the parent who will be the primary source of care for the child in the months following birth, adoption, or surrogacy — are entitled to:

- 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave (100% of base salary)
- An additional 8 weeks of parental leave paid at 60% of base salary
- Total maximum leave: 24 weeks

3.2 Secondary Caregiver Leave

Employees designated as the secondary caregiver are entitled to:

- 4 weeks of fully paid parental leave (100% of base salary)

Secondary caregivers may request additional unpaid leave beyond the 4 weeks in consultation with their manager and HR.

3.3 Adoption and Surrogacy

Employees welcoming a child through adoption or surrogacy are entitled to the same leave benefits as biological parents. Primary caregivers in adoption and surrogacy arrangements receive 16 weeks fully paid plus 8 weeks at 60% pay. Secondary caregivers in such arrangements receive 4 weeks fully paid. Meridian Technologies may also provide reasonable paid time off for adoption-related legal proceedings or court appearances, not to exceed 5 business days.

4. RETURN TO WORK

Upon conclusion of parental leave, employees are guaranteed reinstatement to their same role, or to an equivalent position with the same title, compensation, benefits, and responsibilities. Meridian Technologies will not demote, reduce compensation, or alter job responsibilities as a result of an employee taking parental leave.

Employees who wish to return to work on a phased or part-time basis for up to 4 weeks following the end of their leave may request a phased return-to-work arrangement. Such requests are subject to manager and HR approval.

5. BENEFITS CONTINUATION

All employee benefits — including health, dental, and vision insurance; 401(k) contributions; and equity vesting — continue uninterrupted during the full period of paid parental leave. During any period of unpaid leave, employees may elect to continue benefits at their own expense.

6. HOW TO APPLY

Employees must notify HR and their direct manager of their intent to take parental leave no later than 8 weeks before the planned start date of leave. Where circumstances do not allow for advance notice (e.g., premature birth or emergency adoption), employees should notify HR as soon as reasonably practicable.

To initiate the leave request process:
1. Complete the Parental Leave Request Form available on the HR intranet portal
2. Submit supporting documentation (e.g., expected due date, adoption placement letter)
3. Schedule a leave planning meeting with your HR Business Partner

7. CONTACT

For questions about this policy or to begin the leave application process, contact the People Operations team at hr@meridiantech.com or reach out to your assigned HR Business Partner directly.

This policy is subject to applicable federal, state, and local laws. Where law provides greater benefits than this policy, the legal requirement will apply.
