1. INTRODUCTION

At Meridian Technologies, the way we work is as important as the work itself. This Code of Conduct defines the ethical standards and behavioral expectations that apply to all Meridian employees, contractors, and anyone acting on behalf of the company. Adherence to this Code is a condition of employment.

2. CORE VALUES

Everything at Meridian is guided by four core values:

  INTEGRITY — We do what we say, and we say what we mean. We act with honesty and transparency in all interactions, whether with colleagues, customers, or partners.

  INNOVATION — We challenge the status quo, embrace experimentation, and are never satisfied with "good enough." We give ourselves and each other permission to try new things and learn from failure.

  INCLUSION — We believe diverse perspectives produce better outcomes. We actively cultivate a workplace where every person feels valued, heard, and empowered to contribute fully.

  IMPACT — We focus our energy on work that matters. We hold ourselves accountable to meaningful outcomes, not just activity.

3. ANTI-HARASSMENT AND DISCRIMINATION POLICY

Meridian Technologies maintains a zero-tolerance policy for harassment, discrimination, and hostile behavior of any kind. Prohibited conduct includes, but is not limited to:

- Verbal, physical, or visual harassment based on race, color, religion, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, national origin, age, disability, or any other legally protected characteristic
- Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other conduct of a sexual nature
- Creating or contributing to a hostile, intimidating, or offensive work environment

Employees who experience or witness harassment or discrimination are encouraged to report it immediately through the channels described in Section 8. All reports will be investigated promptly and treated with confidentiality to the extent possible.

4. CONFLICT OF INTEREST

Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict — or could reasonably appear to conflict — with the interests of Meridian Technologies. Specific obligations include:

- Employees must disclose any direct or indirect financial investment in a Meridian competitor, customer, or vendor that could influence business decisions
- Employees involved in vendor selection or procurement must not have undisclosed personal or financial relationships with vendor representatives
- Hiring managers may not participate in decisions to hire immediate family members

All potential conflicts must be disclosed in writing to your manager and HR Business Partner. Disclosure does not automatically constitute a violation; failure to disclose does.

5. CONFIDENTIALITY AND TRADE SECRETS

All employees execute a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) upon hire. Key obligations include:

- Do not disclose Meridian's proprietary information, customer data, financial data, product roadmaps, or strategic plans to any outside party without explicit authorization from Legal
- Treat information you access as part of your role with the same discretion you would want applied to your own personal information
- Upon departure from Meridian, all obligations regarding confidential information remain in force as specified in your employment agreement and NDA

Unauthorized disclosure of trade secrets or confidential information may result in legal action in addition to disciplinary measures.

6. SOCIAL MEDIA POLICY

Employees are welcome to use personal social media platforms in their personal capacity. However:

- Do not represent opinions as those of Meridian Technologies unless you have been explicitly authorized to speak on behalf of the company
- Do not post confidential, non-public information about Meridian, its products, customers, or employees
- Use good judgment — public statements can affect Meridian's reputation and your own standing
- Employees who are authorized to manage Meridian's official accounts must follow the additional Social Media Governance Guidelines (available on the HR intranet)

When in doubt, do not post. If you are unsure whether something is appropriate to share, contact communications@meridiantech.com.

7. GIFT AND ENTERTAINMENT POLICY

To maintain the integrity of business relationships, Meridian places limits on the acceptance and giving of gifts:

- Employees may accept gifts valued at no more than $100 from vendors, customers, or business partners
- Gifts above this threshold must be declined or returned, and the offer disclosed to your manager
- Cash gifts of any amount are never acceptable
- All gifts received above $25 in value must be logged in the Gift Disclosure form on the HR intranet

8. REPORTING VIOLATIONS

Meridian encourages all employees to speak up when they observe or experience conduct that may violate this Code or any company policy. Reports can be made through:

- Ethics Hotline: 1-800-555-0190 (available 24/7, anonymous reporting available)
- Email: ethics@meridiantech.com
- Your HR Business Partner or any member of the Legal team
- Your manager (if you are comfortable doing so)

All reports are investigated by the Ethics and Compliance function. Where legally required, reports may be escalated to external authorities.

9. WHISTLEBLOWER PROTECTION

Meridian strictly prohibits retaliation against any employee who reports a concern in good faith. Retaliation — including termination, demotion, harassment, or any adverse employment action in response to a protected report — is itself a violation of this Code and applicable law. Employees who believe they have experienced retaliation should report it immediately to HR or Legal.

10. DISCIPLINARY PROCESS

Violations of this Code are taken seriously and may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. The severity of the response will be commensurate with the nature of the violation, and may include:

- Formal written warning
- Mandatory training
- Suspension (with or without pay)
- Termination of employment
- Referral to law enforcement where criminal conduct is involved

Meridian will conduct a fair and thorough investigation before determining disciplinary outcomes. Employees under investigation are expected to cooperate fully.

This Code of Conduct does not constitute an employment contract and is subject to revision. The most current version is always available on the Meridian HR intranet.
